The Problem

Ever take your canine companion for a nice relaxing day at the beach, spend the afternoon at the local park, or just go out for a quick stroll around the block? If you're a dog owner you might have said yes to more than one of those, and you've probably had at least one of those experiences be less than relaxing; having your dog run up to some kids at the beach and worried the parents will see your sweet Fido as a hellhound and beat him back, or you have had a random dog bolting straight for yours at the park and you can already see the vet bill burning a hole in your wallet or having to pass another pet parent and their pooch pass you on a narrow sidewalk which normally would be fine... But their teacup size pup is starting to look like a chew toy in the eyes of your hound.

Sometimes these outings can lead to heartbreaking incidents that could have been avoided, either someone assumes that their dog is friendly and so yours must be too, or your dog is given a bad name for how they look rather than who they are. And while trying to protect someone else's furbaby from yours can be seen as uncalled for hostility, or in some situations a dog might not get to go and have the same experiences like getting to the park because it is the "safer" option or similar compromises.

At the end of the day, the dog's personality is not the problem, miscommunication, and assumptions. All dog owners know their dog, the problem is no one else does. Your dog might be a social butterfly and want to say hello to everyone and every dog, or maybe they play rough and get mislabelled as aggressive, or just need some time to warm up to others... and just like how some people don't like other people, some dogs don't like other dogs, and that's okay.

So having identified communication as the problem, the next issue is how can you tell other pet parents about your pooch's personality without being close enough to sniff one another and not having to bark from across the park?

The Solution!

You're more likely to find someone who owns a smartphone than someone who has a dog, so to tackle our problem of miscommunication a mobile app which allows users to see which dogs are at the park and have a quick read of their personalities and gauge how well your dog will get along with them, or receive a notification of another dog coming around the corner and others can view your profile and know how best to respect your dog's space and time outside.

The purpose of the app is prevention. Being able to clearly see that a dog is "non-friendly" or that they don't play well with smaller children or dogs, or having something able to check your blindspot can make the difference between life and death, in some cases. To better explain all this I have created a demo of how the app should look and function in more detail which you can find in the How does it work section.

How It (Should) Work

PoochPing (my mom's name for it and I can't think of anything better yet) will be a native app, easily installed and used by anyone, anywhere, and will be very basic in terms of what it will do and why.

When users choose to take their four-legged friend for an outing and allowing access to their location, and I feel the app should only be granted access while activated by the user, PoochPing will use this similarly to how runner apps are used to track progress and accurately track their live location, and at the same time calculate the distance between them and other users. This is used in conjunction with the color collar system to determine if the user should receive a push notification to alert them of a potential trigger for their dog, whether the other user has a smaller breed which your dog should avoid, a nervous dog that you need to be considerate of, or if the dog is listed as "non-friendly" and all in the area need to practice a safe social distance.

The color collar system is a tagging system to assign specific markers to the user so others can clearly see which group their dog belongs to and allow for quick decision-making that can help prevent incidents between both dogs and owners. I have tried to avoid words and colors which may negatively label certain traits and try to be as inclusive and respectful to each group. The term "non-friendly" and playful colors which are the general colors of dog toys have been used to try to accomplish a polite tagging system.

I do understand that these colors may not appeal to all and will develop a 'dark theme' as well as a colorblind-friendly icon system to ensure all can use it and avoid any miscommunication within the app itself.

Green Collar

Easy-going and happy to say hello and play with anyone. But these dogs may come across a bit strong at first, but understand they are just playful.

Pet (User) Profile

The user profile will be more for their canine counterpart than for them. The accounts may be linked to a person via an email address or cellphone when they signup for a PoochPing account, but the details within the user profile should be compiled of the dog's details.

The dog's name, breed, age, and color collar select as well as a short bio to help others get a better idea of their personality and a picture so when close enough others can see who the profile is assigned to. This helps to avoid others judging your dog on their looks alone.

Demo

Leaflet is an open-source JavaScript library mainly used for mobile-friendly interactive maps and is ideal for my project and Stamen Design LLC who work with data visualization and cartography have created a beautiful watercolor base map with OpenStreetMap which is community-owned and aims to give access to free, editable maps of the world which are up-to-date.

I chose Leaflet simply due to its capabilities and how easy it is to customize map features and OpenStreetMap tiles are reliable and beautiful base maps adding accuracy and aesthetic.

                  center: [52.493891, 13.446395],
                  minZoom: 14,
                  zoom: 16,
                });
              
                L.tileLayer("	http://c.tile.stamen.com/watercolor/{z}/{x}/{y}.jpg", {
                  attribution:

                }).addTo(map);

The demo illustrates how I hope the app will function. The user icon seen as a dog paw is a draggable marker for you to interact with the map and its starting location is 52.493891, 13.446395, but you may recognize the location of the address as Lohmühlenstraße 65, 12435 Berlin, Germany.

The other markers have been given set starting points and move around the map to newly assigned random locations to emulate live locations of users updating.

The pink marker is to mimic a user with a pink collar tag and has been given a set radius. Should any marker enter that radius the radius will change color and a notification will appear to alert the user of another dog nearby. 

 
            alertcircle.setLatLng(icons["pinkCollar"]["coords"]);
              var isInside = false;

            });

You can either wait for one of the other markers to enter the radius or drag the user icon closer to the pink marker until the alert is triggered
